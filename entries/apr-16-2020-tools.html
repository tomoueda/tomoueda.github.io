Favorite Tools In Programming

There's a particular ethos to tools in programming. When one uses a hammer, no one knows the origin of the hammer and how humanity came to rely on the tool. In programming, most of the innovators of everyday tools are still alive and well. Often, these prominent programmers serve as a source of inspiration for others, and they shaped the industry in a culturally meaningful way.

Some of the most potent tools are notoriously difficult to master. The difficulty adds a layer of cultural nuance. Upon meeting a programmer, I, perhaps unwisely, gauge their level of competence based on their choice of tools. I want to go through the list of tools that I consider legendary.

Emacs - Text Editor
Emacs is a text editor, and it is so much more. Some of the most legendary programmers still use Emacs to this day, including Jeff Dean and Sanjay Ghemawat, the two pillars of Google, Peter Norvig, Linus Torvald, and of course, Richard Stallman, the developer who made Emacs himself. He even made a parody church off of Emacs and deemed the next infamous text editor on this list the enemy.

Vi(m) - Text Editor
To a devout Emacs follower, Vim is the devil's editor. In Emacs, You could even enable evil-mode, a Vim key-binding. Only prominent rebel programmers like Paul Graham, Larry Wall, the creator of Perl uses Vim. I also use vim :).

Git - Version Control
Git fluency is pretty much a hard requirement to get a job in programming. Git pretty much decimated other version control tools and spawned an entire subsection of the industry. A lot of companies like Github and Gitlab offers Git as a service. Linus Torvalds will consistently show up in every list of essential programmers. Linux is cute, but Git is zen.

Latex - Academic Markup System
Imagine doing your math homework on your computer. Most word processing applications will struggle when you need to exponentiate anything. Most academic documents before Latex was atrocious to read.
