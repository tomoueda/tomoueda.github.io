Don't get the Pixelbook.

... Especially if you're a dev.

So I switched from iPhone to the Pixel a couple of years ago. I've lived in the Apple ecosystem for far too long, and I wanted to try a different platform. The only real alternatives are Samsung, a brand I'm not too fond of, and the Pixel. There was a rough two-week transition period, but I've come to enjoy the Pixel thoroughly and fell in love with the stock Android experience.

I thought maybe; I should be open-minded and give the Pixelbook a similar benefit of the doubt. I regret ever considering it. While the Pixel phone is a fantastic flagship alternative to the iPhone, the same cannot be said about the Pixelbook. Chromebooks are terrible for developers.

In Apple's ecosystem, you get terrific terminal apps like Iterm2 and Hyper. For video editing, you could either go with Adobe or Final Cut Pro. For text editors, I know most developers use applications like Sublime or VsCode. One would think that a developer-centric company like Google would build a machine for its developers, but I'm dismayed to tell you all this is a consumer-level product. The built-in terminal app is terrible. It's frustrating to try and get anything done. I tried editing videos, but there aren't any tolerable applications on the Android Appstore.

If you're going to sell a high-end machine, the laptop has to come with tools for the creator. I know the users who are most devoted to the Apple ecosystem are developers and video editors. The extra 300 dollars you'll spend on the Macbook Air will go a long way considering the cognitive ease.
